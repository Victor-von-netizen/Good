Don'ts in Rwanda

This section highlights actions and behaviors to avoid to show respect for Rwandan culture.

Don'ts in Rwanda

  • Don’t Wear Inappropriate Clothing (We won’t arrest you, but we *will* judge!)

    This tip covers dressing appropriately. And yes, simon will judge you.

  • Don’t Point Fingers at People (Unless you’re ready for the “what did I do?” stare)

    Turns out, pointing fingers is seen as disrespectful. Remember to keep your hands in check!

  • Don’t Speak Without Thought About Sensitive Topics (Educate yourself first)

    Instead of voicing uninformed opinions, visit national memorial museums and gain crucial insights into Rwanda's history and culture.

  • Don’t Smoke in Public (This isn’t the 1950s, darling)

    Public smoking is a big no-no in Rwanda. Save it for designated areas unless you like hefty fines and disapproving glances.

  • Don’t Litter (Or prepare your wallet!)

    Littering is a no-go here. Not only is it frowned upon, but it’s also punishable by law. Better to save those coins for coffee!

  • Don’t Be Too Loud (Unless there’s a game, then scream away!)

    Rwandans generally keep things calm and quiet—except at bars during matches. Then all bets are off, and volume levels are fair game!

  • Don’t Spit in Public (It’s not the Wild West!)

    Spitting in public isn’t just unhygienic; it’s also considered rude. Keep your spit to yourself—trust us, everyone will thank you.
